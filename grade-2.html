Grade 2 —
The Widening Circle

Central question: Who do we include in our circle of care, and can we keep expanding it?

Grade 2 is a year of expansion. Students take everything planted in Grade 1 and ask how far it can reach. Through meditation, story, drawing, and prayer, they extend warmth step by step to family, friends, animals, and all beings.

01 · The year's shape

Care expands, ring by ring

The year moves from inward to outward, then back to the question of help. Unit 1 begins with the child's own heart and expands compassion one ring at a time. Unit 2 follows the transformation of Prince Siddhartha. Unit 3 asks how a teacher reaches people who are afraid or stuck.

06 · What Grade 2 plants

Practices that continue for years

Sem kye and Ngowa become the standing opening and closing practice through Grade 8. The circles of compassion return in Grades 5 and 7; the Buddha's life deepens in Grades 4 and 7; Guru Rinpoche's story and the Seven-Line Prayer continue in Grades 3 and 5.
