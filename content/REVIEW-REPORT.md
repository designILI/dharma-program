# Dharma Program — Curriculum Quality Review
*Conducted 2026-07-20 · Full curriculum: 152 lessons, Grades 1–8*

Three review agents worked in parallel across the full lesson corpus, reading lesson files directly from the file system and checking against the standing build rules, schema specification, and narrative design constraints. This document synthesises their findings.

---

## How to Use This Report

Issues are grouped into three tiers:

- **🔴 MUST FIX** — structural errors, build rule violations, or narrative continuity breaks that will cause real problems in the classroom or undermine the curriculum's design integrity. Fix before use.
- **🟡 SHOULD FIX** — inconsistencies, departures from spec, or editorial roughness that will be noticeable to a trained teacher. Fix before printing/distribution.
- **🟢 CONSIDER** — minor points, judgment calls, or questions for the curriculum author to decide. Fix or acknowledge intentionally.

Each item cites specific files and fields.

## What Is Working Well

Before the issues: the curriculum is substantially strong. Across 152 lessons, the following are consistent achievements:

**Schema compliance** — All 13 fields appear in the correct order in every reviewed file. This is a significant accomplishment across the corpus.

**FEEL objectives** — Uniformly specific, honest, and phenomenologically precise. Not a single vague "feel happy" objective found across the full curriculum. The Grade 6–8 FEEL objectives in particular ("the specific combination of exhaustion, conviction, and open-ended hope that characterises asking a third time after two refusals") are among the strongest features of the document. This is the standard: maintain it.

**BE ABLE TO objectives** — Consistently describe the lesson's invitation rather than the ideal student output. The distinction is well-maintained throughout.

**Meaning-first prayer introductions (Grades 2–7)** — Sem kye and Ngowa (G2), Dechen Monlam (G5), Dolma/Tara Prayer (G6), and Rewo Sang Chod (G7) are all introduced with the meaning-first protocol correctly applied. The Grade 6 introduction names the protocol explicitly ("meaning first — always meaning first"), which is a fine model. G2.U3.C3 (Seven-Line Prayer line-by-line) is an exemplar.

**Pajapati arc (G8.U4)** — The seven-class novel study is complete, correctly sequenced, and thematically unified. The three refusals are placed correctly in C5 (not before). The Tara-Pajapati connection lands well. Historically accurate throughout.

**Body moments** — Across all grades, body moments are specific, brief, and genuinely connected to the lesson's conceptual core. They are not generic mindfulness inserts. G8.U4.C5's walk across the room, G4.U1.C1's open hand / closed fist, G7.U1.C1's ten-second powerlessness hold — these encode the teaching in the body.

**Plan B** — Present and well-targeted in every logistics-dependent lesson reviewed. Plans B identify the specific failure mode and provide a genuine alternative, not a hedge.

**Teacher Notes** — The two-part structure (Curriculum guidance / My reflection) is consistent throughout. Curriculum guidance sections are substantive and give real forward direction.

**Spiral connections** — Where lesson IDs are cited (e.g., G4.U3.C2 citing G1.U1.C3 explicitly), the connections are genuinely useful teaching tools, not retrospective glosses.

**No teacher names or student counts (Grades 2–8)** — This rule is clean throughout the Grade 2–8 corpus.

## 🔴 MUST FIX

### 1. Pajapati scope violations — G6.U2.C2 and G6.U3.C2

**The most significant content design error in the curriculum.**

The Pajapati thread is the curriculum's most carefully controlled narrative arc (seed → grief touchpoint → partial story → full telling). The specification is precise:
- G6.U2.C2: a *brief, weighted grief touchpoint* — one paragraph naming her grief, no more.
- G6.U3.C2: *partial story only* — early life and historical constraint, without the three refusals, ordination, or death.

**What the files actually contain:**

*G6.U2.C2* contains a multi-paragraph narrative telling the complete ordination story: Pajapati's background, raising Siddhartha, his departure, her becoming a lay practitioner, her husband's death, her first approach to the Buddha, **the three refusals**, the barefoot walk, Ananda's intervention, the ordination, her enlightenment, and a brief mention that "Grade 8 holds her full story." This is not a grief touchpoint. It is the complete narrative. The FEEL objective is correctly calibrated to a brief touchpoint ("the weight of hearing a name become a real person"), making the gap between the FEEL spec and the lesson body clearly visible.

*G6.U3.C2* tells the complete story again in even more detail, including her death: "When she was dying, the Buddha came to her. She died in his presence, having completed the path she had walked toward for decades." The Debrief and Teacher Notes both announce that "her dying is held for Grade 8" — but the lesson body has already told it. This is a direct, in-file contradiction.

**Why this matters:** The arc collapses. By the end of Grade 6, students have heard the complete story twice. Grade 8 Unit 4's seven-class novel study — the curriculum's culminating study, designed around the weight of finally telling the story after six years of carrying the name — has nothing left to reveal. The whole preparation has been given away.

**Fix:**
- *G6.U2.C2*: Trim Pajapati section to one paragraph. Name her, name her grief at Siddhartha's departure, note her perseverance. Do not include the three refusals, ordination, enlightenment, or death.
- *G6.U3.C2*: Trim Pajapati section to early life and the fact of the historical constraint she faced (she wanted to practice fully and faced obstacles) — without naming the three refusals, their outcome, Ananda's role, her ordination, her enlightenment, or her death.

### 2. Prayer sequence errors in Grade 8 Units 4 and 5

**Two distinct errors, both in the most ceremonially significant lessons in the curriculum.**

**Error A — Tsog Due pa displaced in G8.U4.C1–C7 and G8.U5.C5**

Tsog Due pa is the 13th and final prayer in the cumulative ladder, introduced in G8.U1.C1 where it is explicitly called "the last prayer." Its correct position in the full sequence is last — after Rewo Sang Chod.

Beginning with G8.U4.C1 and running through all 7 lessons of G8.U4 plus G8.U5.C5, the prayer moves to the 7th position — mid-sequence, between the Seven-Line Guru Rinpoche Prayer and the Sevenline extended form. This is a production error (almost certainly a copy/paste reordering during authoring) that persists in 16 instances across 8 files.

A teacher who taught G8.U1–U3 correctly would encounter the sequence change in G8.U4.C1 with no explanation. More importantly: G8.U5.C5 (the Threshold ceremony) opens with this incorrect sequence — meaning the curriculum's most symbolically charged act (the full accumulated prayer ladder) is presented in the wrong order.

**Error B — Food Offering Prayer missing from G8.U5.C5**

The Threshold ceremony's teacher notes explicitly state students will lead "the complete 13-prayer set." The prayer list in G8.U5.C5 contains 12 prayers. The Food Offering Prayer (Grade 3's addition, the 7th prayer in the ladder) is absent.

Additionally: the Grade 8 Unit 4 Closing prayer lists put Food Offering Prayer in the final position (after Rewo Sang Chod), when its ladder-correct position is between Seven-Line Guru Rinpoche Prayer and Sevenline extended form. Grade 7 files have this correct; Grade 8 Unit 4 files do not.

**Fix:**
- In all 8 affected files (G8.U4.C1–C7, G8.U5.C5): restore Tsog Due pa to last position in every Opening and Closing prayer sequence.
- In G8.U5.C5 specifically: insert Food Offering Prayer in its correct ladder position (after Seven-Line Guru Rinpoche Prayer, before Sevenline extended form).
- In G8.U4 Closing sequences: move Food Offering Prayer from last position to its ladder-correct mid-position.

**Correct full sequence (for reference):**
1. Kyap Dro
2. Om Mani Padme Hung (7×)
3. Manjushri Prayer
4. Sem kye
5. Ngowa
6. Seven-Line Guru Rinpoche Prayer (standard)
7. Food Offering Prayer
8. Sevenline extended form
9. Dusum Sangay
10. Dechen Monlam
11. Dolma / Tara Prayer
12. Rewo Sang Chod
13. Tsog Due pa ← always last

### 3. Blank Curriculum guidance — 15 Grade 1 files

All 13-field lesson files have a Teacher Notes section with two required parts: "Curriculum guidance" (spiral connections, pedagogical framing) and "My reflection" (blank). In 15 of the 27 Grade 1 lesson files, the Curriculum guidance section reads only "Carry forward: ___" — empty.

**Affected files:** G1.U2.C1–C4, G1.U3.C1–C3, G1.U4.C1–C3, G1.U6.C1–C5.

Grade 1 Unit 1 files (C1–C6) have complete Curriculum guidance; the blank pattern begins with Unit 2 and persists through the Jataka Library. Grade 2 and Grade 3 files are fully written. This appears to be an incomplete authoring pass.

These files are not classroom-ready without Curriculum guidance. A teacher reading G1.U2.C1 has no information about what spiral threads to name, how this connects to later curriculum, or what to watch for pedagogically.

### 4. Teacher pronouns (he/him/his) in four Grade 1 files

The build rule requires "the teacher" with no gendered pronouns. Four Grade 1 files use male pronouns throughout, indicating they were drafted with a specific person in mind:

- **G1.U3.C1:** "The large lotus image is already visible behind *him*." / "*He* does not call attention to it yet." / "the teacher takes the illustrated biography. *He* holds it..."
- **G1.U3.C2:** "the teacher sits. This section is a conversation, not a lecture — but *he* leads it with intention." / "[the teacher inserts *his* own one or two sentences here]"
- **G1.U3.C4:** "the teacher chants the 7-line Guru Rinpoche Prayer once, in full, from memory. *He* chants it as prayer."
- **G1.U4.C1:** "the teacher stands or sits forward. *He* uses the illustrated text as a visual anchor but tells the story in *his* own voice. The cup of water is on *his* desk."

Approximately 15–20 individual pronoun instances across these four files. Replace with "the teacher" throughout.

### 5. Specific student count ("fourteen") in five Grade 1 files

The build rule prohibits specific student numbers. The number "fourteen" is embedded as a class-size reference in five Grade 1 files:

- **G1.U1.C1:** "All *fourteen* of you, and me. That sound was real."
- **G1.U1.C2:** "All *fourteen* students hold up fingers alongside."
- **G1.U1.C6:** "After all *fourteen* flags are hung, the teacher hangs their own."
- **G1.U3.C4:** "*Fourteen* different ways. *Fourteen* minds, *fourteen* hands, one story." Also in Parent Connection: "*fourteen* drawings on the wall."
- **G1.U5.C4:** "There are *fourteen* pages — one for each person in this class."

Replace with "each student," "the class," "every student's flag," "as many pages as there are students," etc.

### 6. Prayer introduction — Kyap Dro, Om Mani Padme Hung, Manjushri Prayer (G1.U1.C1)

The meaning-first protocol requires meaning to be given before students hear or chant a prayer for the first time.

**G1.U1.C1 introduces all three Grade 1 prayers sound-first.** In the Opening, the teacher recites all three prayers before any explanation of their names or content. Om Mani Padme Hung is chanted together with students multiple times before the Mini Lesson gives meaning. The Manjushri Prayer appears to have no designated meaning-introduction lesson anywhere in Grade 1.

The Cognitive Principle field of G1.U1.C1 makes an explicit argument for a sound-first approach: "Students encode the mantra at multiple levels before they know a single thing 'about' it — so the knowing that comes later lands on ground that is already prepared." The lesson is internally coherent with this philosophy. But it directly contradicts the build rule.

**Resolution required:** Either (a) revise the lesson to give meaning before the chanting for each of the three prayers, or (b) formally document that G1.U1.C1 operates under a deliberate sound-first exception, with written justification, so future teachers understand the design decision rather than encountering an apparent inconsistency. Option (b) would require annotating the Teacher Notes accordingly.

### 7. Bloom's level violations — Grades 1–3

The specification permits only Remember / Understand / Apply for Grades 1–3. The following files claim levels outside the band:

| File | Claimed Level | Issue |
|------|-------------|-------|
| G3.U1.C6 | **Analyze** (unqualified, leads the field) | Only G1–3 file to claim Analyze outright without qualifier |
| G2.U1.C7 | **Analyze / Evaluate** | Both Analyze and Evaluate appear; Grade 2 |
| G3.U2.C4 | Apply / **Analyze** | Analyze as full co-claim |
| G1.U1.C6 | Apply / **Evaluate** | Evaluate with qualifier ("in the simple form available to six-year-olds") |
| G1.U2.C4 | Apply "touching **Evaluate**" | Parenthetical but labelled |
| G3.U3.C4 | "**early Evaluate** is present" | Qualified but Evaluate is named |

Additionally, an "early Analyze" pattern appears across many Grade 2 and 3 files (G2.U1.C4, G2.U1.C5, G2.U1.C6, G2.U2.C5, G2.U3.C4, G3.U1.C2, G3.U1.C3, G3.U1.C5, G3.U2.C1, G3.U2.C3). These are hedged claims — the qualifier "early" signals the author is not claiming full Analyze — and may represent legitimate description. The GRADE-3-OVERVIEW.md frames the year as "Apply / Growing Analyze," which seeds this pattern.

Recommend: revise the six files in the table above to stay within the band. For the "early Analyze" pattern, decide as curriculum author whether to retain the qualifier consistently or eliminate it.

### 9. G6.U4.C3 timing — Mini Lesson runs 20 minutes

The Mini Lesson specification is ~10–15 minutes. G6.U4.C3 labels its Mini Lesson as "20 minutes," bringing total planned time to approximately 46 minutes against a 40-minute class. ### 10. FEEL objectives — two vague instances in Grade 1

Two Grade 1 FEEL objectives fail the specificity standard:

- **G1.U6.C5:** "the warmth and belonging that open-heartedness creates — both for the giver and for those received." "Warmth and belonging" is a hoped-for outcome, not an honest emotional condition the lesson creates. Rewrite.
- **G1.U3.C1:** "genuine wonder and a little of the hush that comes when something beautiful and strange is described well. The emotional register to aim for: the room goes quiet in a good way." This describes the desired teaching atmosphere, not students' felt experience. Rewrite.

### 11. Spiral connection formatting inconsistency

The specification requires spiral connections to cite lesson IDs in G#.U#.C# format. In practice, formats vary across the corpus:

- Full ID format ✓ (correct): "G1.U4.C1" — found in Grade 3 and some Grade 4–6 files
- Abbreviated without grade prefix ✗: "U1 C1" — found in Grade 2 files
- Grade-only ✗: "In Grade 2, you learned about Losar" — no unit or class ID
- Concept-only ✗: "connects directly to Grade 2's work on Bodhichitta" — no lesson ID at all
- Forward-reference without ID ✗: "connects forward to Grade 5's study of Tonglen"

Inconsistency is most pronounced in Grade 1–2 files and some Grade 6 Curriculum guidance notes. A global edit pass to standardise to G#.U#.C# would make the references useful for new teachers. The Grade 3 files are the model.

## 🟢 CONSIDER

### 16. G1.U1.C1 and G1.U6.C1 — same story used twice in Grade 1

The Brave Little Parrot appears in G1.U4.C1 (Ahimsa unit, as the anchor lesson) and again in G1.U6.C1 (Jataka Library, standalone session). Students hear the same story twice in the same year. If the tellings are sufficiently differentiated (different lens, different discussion questions, different activity), this is defensible. If they are substantially the same, consider replacing G1.U6.C1 with a different Jataka tale given students already encountered the parrot in Unit 4.

### 17. G2.U1.C1 — "starting today" ambiguity

The Opening note says "Starting today, we add two new prayers to our opening and closing" — but the two new prayers (Sem kye and Ngowa) don't actually appear until C3. The "starting today" refers to the student-leader role, not the new prayers. Minor editorial ambiguity; could confuse a teacher preparing for C1.

---

### 18. Seven-Line Prayer grade assignment — G2 vs. G3

The specification assigns the Seven-Line Guru Rinpoche Prayer to Grade 3 with a meaning-first introduction. The actual curriculum introduces it across three grades:
- G1.U3.C4: sound exposure only ("just receive it")
- G2.U3.C3: full meaning-first introduction, line by line — this IS the meaning-first introduction
- G3.U1.C1: memorisation (meaning already given in Grade 2)

The meaning-first protocol IS followed — just in Grade 2 rather than Grade 3. Whether to update the spec to "G2 introduces meaning → G3 completes memorisation," or to move the meaning-first introduction back to Grade 3 (making G2 a sound-exposure-only year), is a curriculum design decision for the author to make and document.

---

### 19. Food Offering Prayer introduction — meaning-alongside

The Food Offering Prayer introduction (G3.U1.C3) gives meaning alongside chanting ("pausing briefly to give a plain-language gloss of each line") rather than strictly before. This is meaning-alongside rather than meaning-before. Confirm whether this satisfies the meaning-first protocol or whether the lesson should be revised to give the full meaning before the first chant.

---

### 20. G1.U4.C4 (The Rescue) — Plan B weaker than Grade 3 standard

G1.U4.C4 sends students outside to find, handle, and release live insects. The Plan B is partial: "If you cannot find insects in the schoolyard, have a Plan B: one insect per table group rather than one per student." This does not cover the case where the outdoor component must be cancelled entirely. Grade 3's Tsethar release (G3.U1.C5) is the gold standard: it has a full, labeled Plan B covering postponement, rescheduling, and a substitute guided visualization. G1.U4.C4 should reach the same standard given students are handling living creatures and leaving the classroom.

### 21. G5.U3.C3 — teacher identity note

The Mini Lesson contains: "And I received from [my teacher's name, if the teacher is comfortable sharing it]." This is a placeholder in brackets, not a hardcoded name. The Teacher Notes clarify the invitation is optional. No build rule violation — but confirm the school is aware this is a judgment call left to individual teachers.
