## Style-Lock Note & Standing Build Rules

Distilled from the exemplar lesson (Grade 6 / Bardo / Class 3) and updated after the Grade 1 critique.

**Voice:** Warm and direct teacher-to-teacher; written as if the author is standing beside you in the room. No jargon without definition. No sentimentality — but genuine feeling is honoured and created conditions for.

**Depth:** Every section is complete enough that a substitute teacher, reading cold, could deliver the lesson with integrity. Exact teacher language is quoted where it matters most: the retrieval prompt, the Turn-and-Talk prompt, the debrief question, the "so what" moment. Everything else is direction, not script.

**Rhythm:** The lesson breathes. Each section moves — settle → remember → hear → talk → make → surface → close. There is always forward motion and always a pause. The activity is never passive.

**The test applied to every lesson before saving:**
> *"If taught exactly as written, would a child leave having encountered something real — a genuine story, a real practice, an honest moment of reflection — or having been taught at?"*
If the answer is "taught at," the lesson is revised before the file is saved.

---

### Standing build rules — apply from Grade 2 onwards

**File header:** No teacher name. No student count. Format: `*Duration: 40 minutes*` only.

**Within lesson body:** Never name a specific teacher. Use "the teacher" throughout. Never reference a specific student count ("14 students," "all 14"). Use "the class," "each student," "one per student."

**Student names:** Never use specific student names as examples (e.g., in debrief modelling). Use "this student," "one child," etc.

**FEEL objective:** Must name a specific, honest emotional condition the lesson's design actually creates — not a hoped-for atmosphere. "A sense of wonder" is too vague. "The gentle surprise of making a sound together and noticing it in your own body — something between wonder and settledness" is the standard.

**BE ABLE TO objective:** Describe what the lesson *invites*, not an ideal output. If the lesson protects students who don't participate from being singled out, the objective cannot demand participation. Write "will feel welcome to…" or "some will…" where appropriate.

**Teacher Notes format — two sections, always:**
```
### Teacher Notes

**Curriculum guidance** *(read before class — written by curriculum author):*
Carry forward: [author's notes on what to carry, connections to next class, what to watch for]

**My reflection** *(complete after class):*
Timing: ___
Energy & engagement: ___
Students to follow up: ___
What to change: ___
```
The "Curriculum guidance / Carry forward" field is pre-filled by the curriculum author where content exists; left as ___ where it does not. The "My reflection" fields are always blank — the teacher fills them in after class.

**Plan B:** Any lesson with a logistics-dependent or materials-dependent activity (outdoor, live creatures, special materials) must include a visible Plan B early in the Materials Checklist — not buried at the bottom.

**Oral storytelling:** Jataka sessions and any lesson requiring oral telling must include a note that the written retelling can be read verbatim. Teachers should not feel obligated to memorise.

**Spiral connections:** When a lesson builds on content from a lower grade, name the connection explicitly in the Curriculum Guidance field (e.g., "Spiral note: students met Om Mani Padme Hung in Grade 1, Unit 1 — retrieve that memory before introducing its meaning here").

**Grade-level overview:** Each grade folder must include a `GRADE-[N]-OVERVIEW.md` file created at the same time as the lessons, not after.

**Prayer reference:** The correct prayer set for each grade is in `/curriculum/PRAYER-REFERENCE.md`. Do not reproduce full prayer texts in individual lesson files — reference them by name only.
