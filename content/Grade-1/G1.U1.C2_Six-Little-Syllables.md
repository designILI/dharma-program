# G1.U1.C2 — Six Little Syllables
## Grade 1 · Unit 1 · Class 2 of 6 · Six Little Syllables
*Duration: 40 minutes*

**Topic (Concrete and Invisible):** Six syllables counted on fingers → each syllable as a small door opening toward a quality of a kind heart

**Cognitive Principle Featured:** Elaborative Encoding — meaning sticks when it is connected to something a child already knows and cares about. Each syllable of Om Mani Padme Hung is given a child-language pointer (not a definition), tethered to concrete experiences students have already had: a jewel, a flower that grows in muddy water, a wish. The meaning does not need to be exact; it needs to be felt as real.

**Bloom's Level:** Remember and Understand — students remember the sound from C1 and now begin to build meaning around it. Understanding at this age is not verbal comprehension; it is felt correspondence. "Oh — that sound points toward that."

**Prior Class Summary:** In C1, students heard Om Mani Padme Hung for the first time and chanted it together three rounds — once led by teacher, once by students alone, once together. They were given the year's essential question — "How can a wish travel to someone far away?" — and sent away without an answer. The sound is in them. The question is in them. Today builds meaning on both.

---

### Learning Objectives

Students will **KNOW** the six syllables of Om Mani Padme Hung by name and in sequence, and one child-language pointer for each.

Students will **UNDERSTAND** that the mantra is not just a sound — each piece of it reaches toward something: a quality of kindness, like a jewel or a lotus.

Students will **BE ABLE TO** chant the mantra and hold up one finger per syllable, naming each with its simple pointer.

Students will **FEEL** a growing sense of ownership over the mantra — that this is theirs to carry, not just something to recite.

---

### Materials Checklist

- *Moody Cow Meditates* by Kerry Lee MacLean (1 copy) — read the full book today
- Singing bowl or hand bell (1)
- Six large cards — one per syllable (OM, MA, NI, PAD, ME, HUNG) — each with the syllable written in large letters on one side and a simple illustration on the other:
  - OM → a glowing sunrise or a light bulb (body, speech, mind becoming kind)
  - MA → a small jewel or gem (the precious wish)
  - NI → a jewel in the lotus (the wish inside the heart)
  - PAD → a lotus flower (something beautiful from muddy water)
  - ME → the lotus in full bloom (the wish fully open)
  - HUNG → a small figure with open arms (all beings)
  - **Advance prep:** These cards can be hand-drawn on card stock. Illustrations need not be beautiful — they need to be clear. Prepare them the night before. Each card approximately A5 size (half a sheet of paper), so children can see from across the circle.
- Small basket or tray to hold the cards face-down before reveal
- No student materials needed today

**Room setup:** Circle for the full lesson. Cards arranged face-down in the centre of the circle, resting on the tray beside the singing bowl. Students will not know what the cards are when they arrive.

**Student leader:** Select the leader before class. Brief them: *"You will lead our opening and closing prayers today. I'll stand beside you and help if you need it. Just try your best — there is no wrong way."*

---

### Lesson Body

#### Opening — 5 minutes

Students enter the circle. The tray of face-down cards is visible. Do not explain it.

Today's student leader comes to stand beside the teacher at one point in the circle. the teacher nods to indicate: begin.

**Kyap Dro (Refuge):** Leader recites, the teacher chants alongside softly. If the leader falters, the teacher carries the line without drawing attention to the gap.

**Om Mani Padme Hung:** Leader chants, seven times. Whole class joins. By now, most students will know the rhythm from C1. the teacher listens more than leads, visibly pleased by the class's voice.

**Manjushri Prayer:** Leader recites, the teacher alongside.

Strike the bowl once to close.

*"Thank you."* — to the leader, briefly. Do not make it into a performance. The leader sits.

---

#### Prior Knowledge Activation — 5–8 minutes

*"Last class, we said something together — a sound. Can anyone remember it?"*

Wait. Do not prompt with the first syllable. Trust the encoding from C1.

**Strong-response example:** A child says "Om Mani Padme Hung!" the teacher smiles and says: *"Yes. Let's all say it together — once, slowly."* The class chants it together, once. *"Good. You remembered."*

**Surface response:** A child says "Om..." and trails off. *"Let's help each other — anyone? What comes after Om?"* Invite the group to fill in. This is collaborative retrieval, not correction.

**If no one remembers:** the teacher says: *"Close your eyes for a moment. Think back to last class — we made a sound together. Can you hear it in your memory?"* Pause. Then begin chanting softly yourself — Om... — and let students recognise and join in. *"There it is. It was there the whole time."*

After retrieval: *"I have a question. We said this sound is like a wish. Can anyone think of something they've wished for? Not a big wish — just any wish."*

Let several students respond briefly. Receive each one warmly. Do not rank them (don't say "oh that's a beautiful wish"). Simply listen.

*"Today we're going to find out what each little piece of this mantra is wishing for."*

---

#### Mini Lesson — 10–15 minutes

*"Remember I told you last time — Om Mani Padme Hung has six pieces. Let's count them again."*

Chant slowly, holding up one finger per syllable. All students hold up fingers alongside. OM. MA. NI. PAD. ME. HUNG. Six fingers.

*"Six little sounds. Six little doors. Each one opens toward something."*

the teacher lifts the first card from the tray, still face-down.

*"Ready? First one."* Turn the card: **OM** — with the sunrise illustration.

*"Om. This one points toward something big — the body and the voice and the mind of the kindest person who ever lived. The Buddha. When we say Om, we're pointing in that direction. Like pointing at a mountain you can't quite see yet but you know is there."*

Pause. Let students look at the card. Do not rush to the next.

*"Say it with me: Om."* Students say it once.

Continue through each syllable, revealing one card at a time, with the following pointers — spoken in your own natural voice, not read from notes:

**MA** (jewel illustration): *"Mani means jewel. Not the kind you wear — the kind that grants wishes. Imagine something so precious, just holding it makes things better. That's what Mani points to — the jewel of compassion, which is a fancy word for the kind of love that wants everyone to stop hurting."*

**NI** (jewel in lotus): *"Ni — it's the jewel sitting inside the lotus flower. The wish sitting right inside the heart. Sometimes you have something precious and you don't even know it's there. Ni is about that."*

**PAD** (lotus bud): *"Padme — that means lotus. Has anyone ever seen a lotus flower? It grows in muddy, dirty water. But the flower is clean and beautiful. That's why Buddhists love the lotus — it says: even if things around you are hard and muddy, you can still be beautiful inside."*

**ME** (open lotus): *"Me — the lotus opening. Fully open. The wish fully awake. Ready."*

**HUNG** (open arms figure): *"Hung — this is the sound of sending it out. All of it — the jewel, the lotus, the wish — going out to everyone. Every person, every animal, every being."* Spread your arms slightly as you say this.

Now hold up all six cards, one in each hand and the others propped or held by a student helper.

*"So when we say Om Mani Padme Hung — just those six sounds — we're saying: may the jewel of kindness open like a lotus and go to everyone. That's what we're saying."*

Chant it once together, very slowly, while the teacher gestures to each card in turn.

**Vocabulary Moment:** *"Compassion — that's the word Mani points toward. It's not the same as being nice. Compassion means: I see that you are hurting, and I want you to stop hurting. You can feel it for someone even when they're far away and you can't do anything to help them. The mantra is one way we do that."*

**Body Moment:** Place both hands flat on your chest, over your heart. Guide students to do the same.

*"Say it with me, with your hands right here."*

Chant Om Mani Padme Hung, seven times, hands on hearts. The vibration in the chest is audible at close range — some students will notice. One or two might smile in surprise. Let them.

**Emotional Hook:** You are building a sense of the mantra as something that belongs to the children, not something being handed down at them. Watch for the child who makes an independent gesture — adjusts their hand position, closes their eyes, sways slightly. That child is in. Do not point to them. Just notice.

---

#### Turn and Talk — 3–5 minutes

Pairs as established in C1 (keep pairs stable across the unit).

*"Turn to your partner and tell them: which syllable did you like most? You don't have to know why — just tell them which one felt interesting to you."*

Circulate and listen. You will hear a mix of: sound preference (children attracted to the sonority of "hung" or "padme"), image preference (children drawn to the jewel or the lotus), and feeling preference (children who respond to the image of sending kindness to everyone). All are valid entry points. Make a mental note of which children chose which — this will inform how you speak to them individually during C4's painting activity.

Signal with the bowl. Invite one or two pairs to share.

---

#### Activity — 15–20 minutes

*"Now we're going to read the full story from last time. I'll read — you listen. But while I read, I want you to hold the mantra in the back of your mind."*

Read *Moody Cow Meditates* aloud, in full. Recommended reading pace: unhurried, with pauses at illustrations. Do not turn the book to show every page — hold it naturally, sharing pictures when they are especially vivid (the mind jar, the settled expression at the end).

Key pauses:
- When Grandfather sits down with the child: pause and look up. *"Grandfathers who know things."* Continue.
- When the mind jar is introduced: pause and let students look at the image. *"Remember this."*
- At the final settling: allow the silence of the page to register before reading the last line.

After the final page, close the book gently.

*"What happened to the moody cow?"* Let two or three students answer. Keep it brief — you are not deconstructing the story.

*"And what did Grandfather teach him? What was the thing that helped?"*

Students will give various answers: breathing, the jar, being still. Receive them all. Then:

*"The mantra is like that. It's something you do, and while you're doing it, the mud settles."*

Now chant Om Mani Padme Hung together, seven times, as a class. Hands on hearts if students want to — don't require it.

**Scaffold for a student who is confused about the meaning:** After class, or during free time if it arises, take them to the six cards and simply say: *"Pick the one you like."* Then tell them just that card's pointer. One is enough.

**Extension for an early finisher (if time allows):** Ask this student to hold one of the six cards and think of a gesture — just for their syllable. Something that goes with it. They can share it or not. This seeds the movement vocabulary that may emerge in C4.

---

#### Debrief — 5 minutes

*"Here's something interesting. We said this mantra has been said for thousands of years. Thousands. By monks in mountains, by grandmothers, by people you'll never meet. They were all wishing the same thing: may the jewel of kindness open like a lotus and go to everyone."*

Let that land.

*"And now you said it. You added your voice."*

Do not make this into a moral lesson. Do not say "and that means you're kind people." Simply let the image of all those voices — across time, across mountains — hold a moment's space. Then:

*"Our question from last time was: how can a wish travel to someone far away? What do you think now — what's one way?"*

Do not answer it. Let one or two children offer ideas. Receive them without confirming or denying. *"Interesting. Let's keep that question with us."*

---

#### Closing — 3 minutes

Student leader returns to lead the prayer set: Kyap Dro, Om Mani Padme Hung (seven times), Manjushri Prayer.

the teacher chants alongside. Strike the bowl once.

*"Today you learned what each little syllable is pointing toward. Now when you say the mantra, you're carrying those six wishes. Take good care of them."*
