# G8.U5.C2 — The Whole Journey
## Grade 8 · Unit 5 · Class 2 of 5 · The Whole Journey
*Duration: 40 minutes*

**Topic (Concrete and Invisible):** Every making artefact from eight years, held in one room at one time → encountering one's own younger self through the work they made, and recognising the distance travelled
**Cognitive Principle Featured:** Self-referential metacognition — students are not reviewing content today. They are reviewing themselves across time, using their own work as the evidence. The portfolio functions as an external memory that the student examines with the perspective only time and development can provide. What they see in the portfolio is not what they made — it is who they were when they made it.
**Bloom's Level:** Evaluate / Synthesise — students evaluate the arc of their own development across eight years; they synthesise the whole curriculum by holding it as a complete object, visible in their own hands
**Prior Class Summary:** C1 completed the prayer repertoire — all 13 prayers mapped by grade, the body moment of holding the complete set, and the written reflection on the prayer that changed something. Students carry the recognition: the repertoire is complete and it is theirs.

---

### Learning Objectives
Students will **KNOW** every major making artefact from the eight-year curriculum — retrieved from memory before the portfolios are opened, and then confirmed through the portfolios themselves
Students will **UNDERSTAND** what a portfolio is — not as a school record but as a record of a developing mind; what it means to encounter one's own younger self through their own work
Students will **BE ABLE TO** review their own complete portfolio with genuine reflective attention — noticing what surprised them, what they had forgotten, what they are proud of, what they would do differently, and the arc visible now that could not be seen from inside
Students will **FEEL** the specific quality of encountering their own younger self on the page — the recognition of who they were, and the particular texture of the distance the curriculum has moved them

### Lesson Body

#### Opening — 5 minutes
Full Grade 8 prayer set: Kyap Dro · Om Mani Padme Hung (7×) · Manjushri Prayer · Sem kye · Ngowa · Seven-Line Guru Rinpoche Prayer · Tsog Due pa · Sevenline extended form · Dusum Sangay · Dechen Monlam · Dolma / Tara Prayer · Rewo Sang Chod. Student leader rotates.

#### Prior Knowledge Activation — 6 minutes
Teacher: "Today: the portfolio. Before you open it — from memory: list every making artefact you remember, from Grade 1 to Grade 8. Call them out or write them down. Everything you can recall." Students call out — teacher writes on the board, quickly. Allow two or three minutes of active retrieval. Prompt if needed: "Grade 1? Grade 3 — what did we make in Grade 3? Grade 6 — more than one making. Grade 8 — three units of making before today."

The list should grow to include all the major artefacts. Then: "That is what eight years looks like. Before you even open the portfolio. Your memory holds it." Then: "Open the portfolio."

#### Mini Lesson — 3 minutes
Brief framing only: "You are going to spend most of this class with your portfolio — reading it, holding it, noticing what you forgot and what you remember, and what has changed. The question you are holding: *Who was the person who made this, and who are you now?* I am not directing your attention. Your portfolio will direct it. I will be in the room. I will come to you."

---

**Vocabulary Moment:** "The word *portfolio* — from the Latin *portare*, to carry, and *folium*, a leaf. A carrying of leaves. A collection of work carried across time. Your portfolio is a record of your mind — not what you know, but how you have been thinking, making, practising, for eight years. It is also a record of the curriculum's mind: every artefact is a decision the curriculum made about what matters."

---

**Body Moment:** Teacher: "Before you start reviewing — find the artefact you remember making with the most physical attention. The one where your hands were most engaged. Hold it. What do your hands remember about making this?" Sixty seconds. "Hands carry memory differently than minds do. That memory is part of the portfolio too."

---

**Emotional Hook:** The specific quality of encountering one's own younger self through the work they made. This cannot be engineered — it emerges from the contact between the student and their own earlier work. The teacher's role in C2 is to create the conditions for this encounter and then get out of the way.

#### Turn and Talk — Not today
No formal Turn and Talk in C2. The portfolio review is a solo encounter. If students naturally share with a neighbour — "look at this" — allow it. Do not structure it.

#### Activity — 28 minutes
Full portfolio review. Students work through their complete portfolio in order — all eight years of artefacts — at their own pace, making notes on a review sheet. Prompts on the sheet (provided at the start of the activity):

- What surprised me (things I had forgotten, things that looked different than I remembered)
- What I had genuinely forgotten
- What I am proud of
- What I would do differently now
- The arc I can see now that I couldn't see while I was in it — one sentence describing the movement of the portfolio from beginning to end

Teacher circulates quietly. The teacher's role in C2 is to be present without directing. Sit beside a student who seems stuck and ask: "What is in your hands right now?" Let the student describe it. Then leave them alone.

If a student finishes early (unlikely, but possible), offer: "Go back to the beginning. Read it again. What do you see on the second reading that you missed on the first?"

#### Debrief — 4 minutes
Teacher: "What did you see in the arc? Not details — the arc. From the beginning to the end of the portfolio: what moved?" Take three or four responses — listen for what students name as the central development across eight years. Receive everything; do not summarise or evaluate. Then: "Next class: assembling the portfolio for the Threshold. And beginning to write your closing declaration — the statement of what you carry forward. It should be grounded in what you've just held."

#### Closing — 3 minutes
Full prayer set: Kyap Dro · Om Mani Padme Hung (7×) · Manjushri Prayer · Sem kye · Ngowa · Seven-Line Guru Rinpoche Prayer · Tsog Due pa · Sevenline extended form · Dusum Sangay · Dechen Monlam · Dolma / Tara Prayer · Rewo Sang Chod · Food Offering Prayer. Student leader.

Teacher's closing line, specific to today: "You held eight years in your hands today — and the hands that held it are eight years older than the hands that began."

### Teacher Notes

**Curriculum guidance** *(read before class — written by curriculum author):*
Carry forward: The portfolio review in C2 is one of the most powerful classes in the curriculum — and one of the most logistically demanding. It requires that eight years of artefacts are physically present in the room. This is not always simple: some artefacts will have been taken home, some stored by the school, some lost. Begin the logistics well before C2.

The teacher's role in C2 is unusual: it is largely one of witness, not facilitation. The students' encounter with their own portfolios does not need to be shaped or directed. It needs to be held — by a quiet room, a teacher who is present but not intrusive, and enough time. Do not fill the silence in C2 with talk.

Note that there is no Turn and Talk in C2 — this is an exception to the standard schema. The portfolio review is a solo encounter. Students may naturally share discoveries with each other, and this is welcome; but structuring a Turn and Talk would interrupt the quality of attention the activity needs.

After C2, the teacher should review the portfolio review notes (briefly — not for grading, but to understand where each student is before C3). The closing declaration in C3 will be stronger if the teacher knows what each student found most significant in their portfolio review. Take notes if possible.

C3 is the assembly and closing-declaration drafting class — the most writing-intensive class of Unit 5. Give students the full activity time.
