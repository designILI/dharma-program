# G8.U5.C4 — Rehearsing the Presentation
## Grade 8 · Unit 5 · Class 4 of 5 · Rehearsing the Presentation
*Duration: 40 minutes*

**Topic (Concrete and Invisible):** The full ceremony run-through, students leading without the teacher → the gap between rehearsal and real, and what rehearsal reveals
**Cognitive Principle Featured:** Pre-performance embodiment — rehearsal is not repetition. It is the first time the body does the thing, in approximately the conditions of the real. Once the body has done it, the mind's uncertainty decreases; what was imagined is now known. This is especially true for the declaration reading: once a student has read their declaration aloud in front of others, they know what it feels like — and they can refine from that knowledge.
**Bloom's Level:** Create — students lead the full prayer set (synthesis of eight years of practice) and perform their closing declarations and portfolio presentations (synthesis of eight years of making); they receive and incorporate feedback in the service of the real ceremony
**Prior Class Summary:** C3 organised the portfolio and drafted the closing declaration — three parts: what I carry, what changed, what I intend. Students carry their draft declarations and selected artefacts, and the instruction: "What you write today will be read aloud in front of the people who love you. Write something true."

---

### Learning Objectives
Students will **KNOW** the complete sequence of the Threshold ceremony — prayer set → portfolio presentations → closing declarations → Ngowa → Tsog Due pa → teacher's closing word → portfolio distributed
Students will **UNDERSTAND** the distinction between rehearsal and performance; what the body learns from having done something once that the mind cannot learn from anticipation alone
Students will **BE ABLE TO** lead the full prayer set without prompting or assistance from the teacher; deliver their portfolio presentation (one minute) and closing declaration with clarity and appropriate presence; receive and incorporate specific feedback
Students will **FEEL** the gap between rehearsal and the real ceremony — the way doing it once reveals what needs to be refined and what is already settled, and the particular readiness that only rehearsal produces

---

### Materials Checklist
- Teacher's written feedback notes on each student's declaration draft — prepared between C3 and C4, returned at the start of the class
- Student declaration drafts (students should have them)
- Selected portfolio artefacts (students should have their three to five selected pieces)
- Timer — the teacher times portfolio presentations (one minute each) during the rehearsal
- Room arranged for the rehearsal: chairs in approximately the configuration planned for C5 (this matters — the body should learn the spatial arrangement as well as the sequence)
- All C5 logistics confirmed before this class begins (see curriculum guidance)
- **Plan B:** If a student's declaration is not ready for the rehearsal (they have not revised it, or the draft is too thin to work from), do not stop the rehearsal for them. Let them listen to the rehearsal. Then, in the final ten minutes, sit with them and talk through their declaration verbally — transcript what they say — and use that as the working draft for C5. Some students find it easier to speak first and write second.

---

### Lesson Body

#### Opening — 5 minutes (student-led, teacher does not lead)
Full Grade 8 prayer set — led entirely by students. The teacher takes a seat among the group — not at the front, not in a leadership position. The teacher chants with the group but does not lead, cue, or correct during the chanting. This is the first time the students lead the full set without the teacher. Student leaders (one per prayer, or rotating) are determined by the students themselves if possible.

Kyap Dro · Om Mani Padme Hung (7×) · Manjushri Prayer · Sem kye · Ngowa · Seven-Line Guru Rinpoche Prayer · Tsog Due pa · Sevenline extended form · Dusum Sangay · Dechen Monlam · Dolma / Tara Prayer · Rewo Sang Chod.

No Food Offering Prayer in the Opening; it comes in the closing as usual.

#### Prior Knowledge Activation — 3 minutes (after the prayer set, led by students)
Teacher: "How did that feel — to lead without me? What was different?" Take three or four brief responses. Do not analyse — just receive. Then: "Notice what your body knows. That is what eight years of practice produces."

Return teacher feedback notes on declarations. Students read them briefly before the rehearsal. One minute.

#### Mini Lesson — 5 minutes
Teacher: "The Threshold ceremony is structured as follows. We rehearse all of it today."

Frame the ceremony sequence clearly on the board or read it aloud:
1. Full prayer set — student-led; audience participates where they know the prayers
2. Portfolio presentations — each student stands, holds selected artefacts, speaks for one minute
3. Closing declarations — each student reads their three-part declaration in full; silence after each
4. Ngowa — the merit dedication, chanted together; the merit of eight years dedicated outward
5. Tsog Due pa — the feast offering; the closing prayer of the ceremony
6. Teacher's closing word — one sentence prepared in advance; specific to this group
7. Students receive their portfolios — each portfolio returned to its maker; it is theirs

"Today we run through all of it. I will time presentations. I will give brief notes after declarations. We refine between now and C5."

---

**Vocabulary Moment:** "The word *rehearsal* — from the Old French *rehercier*: to harrow again, to go over ground once more. A rehearsal is not practice-in-place-of-real. It is a version of the real that prepares the body and mind for the actual event. The reason we rehearse is that the body needs to have done it once — to have stood up, to have spoken the words aloud, to have felt the silence after the declaration — before the ceremony. What the body knows from doing is different from what the mind imagines from anticipating."

---

**Body Moment:** The rehearsal itself is the body moment. Students stand when they stand, hold artefacts when they present, read declarations from the posture of declaration. The body is fully engaged throughout. There is no separate body moment.

---

**Emotional Hook:** The gap between rehearsal and the real ceremony — the way running through something reveals both what needs refining and what is already settled. The specific readiness that only rehearsal produces: not confidence exactly, but the knowledge that one has done it and survived it and knows what it requires.

#### Activity — 28 minutes (full ceremony rehearsal)
Complete run-through in order. The teacher observes, times, and takes brief notes — but does not intervene during the run-through itself. Corrections come after, not during.

**Step 1 — Prayer set (already done at the Opening, counts as part of the rehearsal).**

**Step 2 — Portfolio presentations (approx. 12 minutes for a class of twelve, or adjust for size):** Each student stands and holds their selected artefacts. One minute per student — teacher times and gives a quiet signal at 55 seconds. Teacher notes: Does the student speak to the audience, or only to the artefact? Is the significance of each artefact communicated, or only described? Is the student present in the body?

**Step 3 — Declaration readings (approx. 8 minutes):** Each student reads their full three-part declaration. After each: five seconds of silence before the next. Teacher notes for each: Is Part 1 specific enough? Is Part 2 honest? Is Part 3 a real intention? Is the reading delivered with enough presence to carry the words?

**Step 4 — Ngowa (3 minutes):** Chanted together. All students lead. Teacher notes: do students know it well enough to lead it without prompting? If not, this is the moment to note and practise.

**Step 5 — Tsog Due pa (3 minutes):** Chanted together. Same note.

**After the run-through — teacher feedback (remaining time):** Specific, brief, direct. Address: (1) timing (any presentations that ran significantly over or under); (2) declaration clarity (which parts need specificity or honesty); (3) prayer set confidence (any prayer that needs one more run-through before C5). Students make revision notes on their declarations.

#### Debrief — 2 minutes
Teacher: "One thing to remember for C5: the ceremony is not about impressing anyone. It is not a performance in the way a school recital is a performance. It is about making something real — making your declaration real by saying it aloud in front of witnesses. Read your declaration as if every word matters — because it does. The people who come to C5 are not coming to be impressed. They are coming to witness."

#### Closing — 3 minutes
Full prayer set — student-led, teacher in the group: Kyap Dro · Om Mani Padme Hung (7×) · Manjushri Prayer · Sem kye · Ngowa · Seven-Line Guru Rinpoche Prayer · Tsog Due pa · Sevenline extended form · Dusum Sangay · Dechen Monlam · Dolma / Tara Prayer · Rewo Sang Chod · Food Offering Prayer.

Teacher's closing line, specific to today — one sentence for the space between rehearsal and the real: "You have done it once. Now you know what it requires."

### Teacher Notes

**Curriculum guidance** *(read before class — written by curriculum author):*
Carry forward: All C5 logistics must be confirmed before C4 begins. If anything is unconfirmed, confirm it in the margin of C4 — but ideally, nothing should be unconfirmed at this stage. The Threshold ceremony in C5 requires:

- **Room arrangement:** Chairs in a circle or horseshoe large enough to include both students and audience. A central surface (a low table, a blanket on the floor, or an altar platform) for portfolio artefacts to be placed as students present. A shrine or altar at the front or centre with an image of the Buddha (or Padmasambhava, or whatever is appropriate to the school's tradition), candles or butter lamps, an offering. The room should feel ceremonial — not like a classroom.
- **Audience invited:** Parents and family members. Teachers (especially teachers who have had this cohort in previous grades). Younger students from the program (especially Grade 6 and 7 — students who will walk this path themselves). Any members of the wider school community who have relevance. Audience members should be told: "This is a ceremony, not a school event. Please plan to be present for the full duration (60–90 minutes). The student declarations are sacred. Please hold silence after each one."
- **Duration:** Allocate 60–90 minutes for C5 — not 40. If the school schedule requires it, the ceremony can begin at a non-standard time.
- **Portfolios:** Each student's complete portfolio, organised and available to be returned at the end of C5. The portfolio goes home with the student — it does not stay with the school.
- **Tsog offerings:** If the school tradition includes physical offerings for a Tsog — food, incense, flowers — these should be prepared before the ceremony.

The teacher's closing word for C5 should be written before C4. It is one sentence, specific to this group — not a general statement about what the curriculum does, but a specific word about who these students are, what they did, and what they carry. The teacher should know, having been with this cohort for the year, what one true sentence would be. Write it before C4. Do not improvise it in C5.
