01

A curriculum that grows
with the child

Hidden Land Dharma is a structured, year-by-year contemplative curriculum spanning Grades 1 through 8. It is not a collection of isolated lessons — it is a single, connected arc. Each grade plants something the next grade will deepen. The mantra a six-year-old first hears in Grade 1 becomes a philosophical inquiry in Grade 8.

Lessons are written so that a substitute teacher, reading cold, could deliver them with integrity. Exact teacher language is given where it matters most. Everything else is direction, not script.

"If taught exactly as written, would a child leave having encountered something real — a genuine story, a real practice, an honest moment of reflection — or having been taught at?"

That question governs every lesson in this curriculum. The answer must always be the first.

02

Three principles behind
every lesson

These commitments are baked into the design — not stated once and forgotten, but present in the structure of every unit, every class, every closing question.

Doing is knowing

Everything is made, moved, or told

At every grade, the highest form of understanding is doing — chanting, drawing, observing, releasing, sharing. Making is never the optional part of a lesson. It is the lesson.

The spiral

Seeds planted early, deepened later

Ideas introduced in Grade 1 return in Grade 5, and again in Grade 8 — each time with greater depth. Teachers are reminded of the spiral in each lesson's guidance notes.

The whole person

Bloom's levels rise across the grades

Grades 1–3 ask children to Remember, Understand, and Apply. Grades 4–6 bring Analysis. Grades 7–8 reach Evaluation and Creation. The curriculum respects where children are.

03

The rhythm of a lesson

Every class breathes through the same arc: Settle → Remember → Hear → Talk → Make → Surface → Close. There is always forward motion, and always a pause. The activity is never passive.

Each lesson runs 40 minutes. The Opening (5 min) and Closing (3 min) are fixed — the prayer set, led by a rotating student leader. If time runs short, the Debrief absorbs the cut. The making activity is protected.

"The teacher's role is not only to transfer knowledge. It is to awaken thinking."

06

The prayer repertoire

Each grade inherits the full prayer set of the grades below it and adds one or two new prayers. By Grade 8, students have been opening and closing class with the same prayers for eight years.

Kyap Dro (G1+) Om Mani Padme Hung (G1+) Manjushri Prayer (G1+) Sem kye, Ngowa (G2+) 7-Line Guru Rinpoche Prayer (G3+) Food Offering Prayer (G3+) Sevenline Prayer, Dusum Sangay (G4+) Dechen Monlam (G5+) Dolma / Tara Prayer (G6+) Rewo Sang Chod (G7+) Tsog Due pa (G8)

For pronunciation, meaning, and context of each prayer, see the Prayer Reference in the curriculum folder.
